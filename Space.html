Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope

Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope (formerly known as the Wide Field InfraRed Survey Telescope (WFIRST)) is a wide field telescope disigned look at large
portions of the night sky at once. While just as sensitive as Hubble it's able to look at a field 100 times wider than Hubble space telescope ever could.
What this allows astronomers to do is observe spots in the sky multiple times over the course of a month instead of every couple of years. This allows follow-up surveys to be done more often on things such as pulsars, flare stars, nebulas, and exoplanets.
Its primary mirror is 2.4 meters in diameter (7.9 feet) and carries multiple instruments. The wide field instrument it's what allows it to look at a wide field of the Sky.
Its Coronagraph instrument allows it to observe exoplanets by measuring the brightness of stars and finding exoplanets in the "dips" of brightness.
It's currently set to launch in 2027 and its primary mission is supposed to last around 10 years.
